Kosgoda turtle hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a non-profit organization located in Sri Lanka. It was founded in 1978 by Mr. Sunil Wickramasinghe, who was inspired to start the hatchery after witnessing the plight of sea turtles in the area. The hatchery is located on a beach in Kosgoda, which is a popular tourist destination. The hatchery is open to the public, and visitors can learn about the conservation of sea turtles and see the turtles in their natural habitat. The hatchery's mission is to protect sea turtles and their eggs. The hatchery does this by collecting sea turtle eggs from the beach and incubating them in a safe environment. Once the turtles hatch, they are released back into the ocean. The hatchery has been very successful in its mission. Over the years, the hatchery has released over 100,000 sea turtles into the ocean. The hatchery has also helped to raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a valuable resource for the conservation of sea turtles. The hatchery is doing important work to protect these amazing creatures, and it is a must-visit for any visitor to Sri Lanka.

Here are some additional details about the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery:

* The hatchery is open from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM, 7 days a week.
* Admission is \$5 for adults and \$2 for children.
* The hatchery is located at 100 Kosgoda Road, Kosgoda, Sri Lanka.
* The hatchery can be reached by bus or train.
* The hatchery offers guided tours, which are available in English, French, German, and Spanish.
* The hatchery also has a gift shop, where you can buy souvenirs and educational materials about sea turtles.

If you are interested in learning more about sea turtles or in supporting the conservation of sea turtles, we encourage you to visit the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery.
